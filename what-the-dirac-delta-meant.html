What the Dirac Delta meant

Published: 18/07/2023
Updated: 18/07/2023
By Arthur Scardua

In statistics.

tags: statistics personal

In previous posts, I shown how the importance of de Dirac Delta in computing the distribution density of a function of random variables, but I missed to show where it came from.

From the dice ideia, the probability of finding two dice which the sum is \(s\) is to sum all the probabilities in which the sum is \(s\). Sounds strange, isn't it? But it is that simple, I guess. If the set of all possible outcomes of two dice is \(\Omega\) and the set of outcomes in which the sum is \(s\) is \(\Omega_s\) , then the final probability is the sum over the intersection of these sets

$$ P(sum=s) = \sum_{i,j \in \Omega \cap \Omega_s} P(face=i) P(face=j) $$

There is another way to writing the same equation, using the Kroeneker delta

$$ P(sum=s) = \sum_{i,j\in\Omega}P(face=i)P(face-j)\delta_{i+j,s} $$

The same ideia applies to the continuous case

$$ \begin{align*} p(y) &= \int_{\Omega \cap \Omega_y} p(x_1)...p(x_i)...p(x_n) dx^n\\ &= \int_\Omega p(x_1)...p(x_i)...p(x_n) \delta\left(y-f(x_1, ..., x_n)\right) dx^n \end{align*} $$

It is related to measure theory. I have to study measure theory ^^".
